Planet forming boundaries
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Inner Disk Edge				| ~0.05 - 0.1 AU			| Too hot for solid materials					
Snow Line (Water Ice Line)	| ~2-3 AU					| Separates rocky planets from gas giants		
Pebble Isolation Line		| ~2-10 AU					| Allows gas giants to accumulate atmospheres	
Ice Line (CO & Methane)		| ~30-50 AU					| Forms icy planets, dwarf planets, comets		
Stability Boundary			| ~100-200 AU				| Outer edge of stable planetary orbits			
Oort Cloud					| ~2,000 - 100,000 AU		| Icy bodies loosely bound to the star			
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

	RH ​= [(a1​+a2​​)/2 × (​m1​+m2​​)/3M]^1/3
--------------------------------------------
a1​,a2​ | semi-major axes of the two planets
m1,m2​ | masses of the two planets
M​ 	  | mass of the central star
RH​    | Mutual Hill Radius
--------------------------------------------

	Δ = (​a2​−a1) / RH​​
---------------------------------------------
Planet Type			  |	Minimum Safe Spacing
----------------------|----------------------
Terrestrial planets	  |	Δ >= 3.5−5 RH​
Gas giants			  |	Δ >= 10 RH
Super-Earths		  |	Δ >= 5−7 RH​
Neptune-like planets  |	Δ >= 7−10 RH​
---------------------------------------------
